LOCAL PRODUCE

Since 1997, we have embedded a unique bond with local vendors. We source out sun dried red chillies, from Pirna North and Cancona south of Goa. Tamarind and Kokum come from harvested trees, once a year.

Palm vinegar prepared by fermenting toddy, is from Goa Velha, goes in preparing red vindaloo and sorpotel masala.

Goa’s minority, very few remaining in this traditional trade, toddy tappers bring in before sunrise sap from coconut trees, straight to our kitchen.

Dry fish delicacy is from Harmal. All staple ingredients comes meticulously ahead in time which goes in preparing our legendary Goan delicacy.

WOMEN WITH RESILIENCE

Suna Nayak joined Mum’s Kitchen after she became a single mother. She had to take care of her two growing children. She works in our kitchen stewarding area and handles mechanised dishwasher.

She is trained to test the dishwasher sometimes failing to do the litmus check of cutlery and crockery sanitised at 80 degree. A test done for quality assurance, in case of standards deviation.

She too is multi skilled also handling our in house laundry. Thanks to Sona. That’s what we call her today.

She is providing us fresh laundered uniform every day. Hope she sets an example to single mothers,for perseverance and resilience to balance both family and work.

She advises single mothers not to lose hope, put your heart in what your doing and one day you will get your dividents in return.
